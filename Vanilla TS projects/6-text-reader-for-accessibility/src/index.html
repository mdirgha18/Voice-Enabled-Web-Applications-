Select text or say: "Pause", "Resume", or "Stop".

🧡 A Story Behind the Reader "Let Every Voice Be Heard, Let Every Word Be Understood" Once upon a screen, in a world flooded with words, there lived countless voices waiting to be heard—not by others, but by themselves. People with visual challenges, attention difficulties, and reading fatigue often stood on the margins, left to scroll silently through oceans of text. The web had grown louder, but for many, the signal was still silent. That’s when the idea sparked: What if the web could speak back? Enter the Text Reader for Accessibility—a gentle companion designed not to replace reading, but to restore access, empower choice, and give voice to words. With the simple tap of a finger or the sound of your voice, this app springs to life, transforming static paragraphs into spoken dialogue, and transforming silence into understanding. You highlight a passage about faraway lands, and it begins to narrate. You say “pause” and the story waits with you. You say “resume” and it flows again—like a friend picking up where they left off. No more squinting. No more skimming. Just a calm voice, clear intention, and the freedom to hear the web the way you need it. Because accessibility isn’t an add-on. It’s a birthright. A design choice. A story worth telling. And this app? It’s one small chapter in a much bigger story— Where every word is not just written... but heard.

Voice commands such as pause, resume, and stop are available to control the speech reading.
